Dr. Manmohan Singh

Member of the Indian National Congress, Singh was the 1st Sikh and 13th prime minister of India.

Time line of Dr Manmohan Singh’s Life

Early Life & Education

  • 1932

    Born on September 26 in Gah, Punjab (now in Pakistan)

  • 1957

    Graduated from Punjab University with a degree in Economics

  • 1962

    Completed his Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Oxford

Early Career

  • 1971

    Appointed Economic Advisor in the Ministry of Foreign Trade, India

  • 1972

    Became Chief Economic Advisor to the Government of India

  • 1982

    Served as Governor of the Reserve Bank of India until 1985

  • 1987

    Appointed Secretary-General of the South Commission in Geneva

Political Career and Achievements

  • 1991

    Became Finance Minister, initiating landmark economic reforms

  • 1998

    Joined the Rajya Sabha as a Congress member

  • 2004

    Sworn in as India's 13th Prime Minister, leading the UPA government

  • 2008

    Signed the historic India-U.S. Civil Nuclear Agreement

  • 2009

    Re-elected as Prime Minister for a 2nd term, focusing on inclusive growth

Later Years

  • 2014

    Completed his tenure as Prime Minister, leaving a legacy

  • 2019

    Returned to the Rajya Sabha as a member from Rajasthan
